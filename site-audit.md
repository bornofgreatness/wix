## The Learning Experience

- Guided, human-centered journey into the lived reality of TBI
- Incomplete public story about TBI (ends in ER; ignores long-term; leaves survivors without clarity)
- Core idea: neurological bottleneck — the world feels too fast, loud, bright, demanding
- What you’ll learn: why the brain becomes overwhelmed; sensory overload; why everyday tasks feel impossible; how to support; how to communicate with clarity and empathy
- Different because: not a lecture / symptom list / medical document — a human translation with emotional intelligence, cognitive accessibility, science, and lived experience
- Disclaimer: awareness only, not medical advice

---

## What TBI Really Is (`/the-truth-about-tbi`)

- Long-term neurological condition, not a momentary event
- Whole-brain effects: cognition, emotion, sensory, communication, memory, executive function, identity
- Symptom spectrum: brain fog ↔ brain on fire; processing difficulty; memory lapses; sensory overload; emotional volatility
- Sensory overload explained as a neurological bottleneck
- Not a character issue — a neurological shift (often misread as unmotivated, emotional, forgetful, etc.)
- Long-term, not temporary; identity-level impact
- “Ahhh Moment” through understanding
- Disclaimer again
